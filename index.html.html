My story

Medicine that takes the time to explain itself.

I didn't grow up planning to be an eye surgeon. It was a pair of glasses at age nine — the first time the world snapped into focus — that made me realize how much sight shapes everything. That moment never really left me.

After my Doctor of Ophthalmology at Buffalo University and surgical training in laser and cataract care at Johns Hopkins, I spent years in high-volume clinics where patients were moved through like a conveyor belt. I started Dr. Kheysin Eye & Vision to do the opposite: to slow down, to explain every option in plain language, and to treat the person, not just the prescription.

Outside the clinic you'll find me on the trails around Lady Bird Lake with my two kids, usually losing a debate about which taco truck is best. I believe the same thing about eyes as I do about most things — the details matter, and you deserve to understand them.
